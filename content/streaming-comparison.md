# How to watch the 2026 FIFA World Cup from the US

The 2026 World Cup runs **June 11 to July 19, 2026** with 104 matches across the US, Canada, and Mexico. You have more streaming options than any prior World Cup, and the choice depends on three things: which language you want, whether you care about watching two matches at the same time, and how much you want to spend.

## The TL;DR

- **English-only, cheapest:** OTA antenna (FOX is free over the air for most US markets) plus Tubi for the opener.
- **Spanish-preferred:** Telemundo OTA (free) or Peacock Premium for Telemundo streaming.
- **All 104 matches, any language, no antenna:** Sling Blue, Hulu + Live TV, YouTube TV, or Fubo. Fubo wins if you care about watching two matches at once.
- **Cheapest streaming-only bundle:** Sling Blue.
- **Most generous on simultaneous-match viewing:** Fubo (customizable 4-game multi-view).

## Pick by use case

**You want to watch the USA opener (June 12) and not much else.**
OTA antenna for free, or Tubi if you're on a laptop. Don't subscribe to anything monthly.

**You're a casual fan and want to catch the matches that look good.**
Sling Blue is the cheapest "all matches in English" path. Cancel after July 19.

**You're a Spanish-language preferred fan or want the Telemundo commentary.**
Peacock Premium ($8 ad-supported) is the cheapest. Or free OTA Telemundo if you have an antenna.

**You're a serious fan who wants to watch two group-stage matches at the same time.**
This matters most during group-stage final days, when up to four matches kick off simultaneously. Fubo is the only consumer-grade bundle with customizable multi-view across up to 4 games on a single screen. YouTube TV also has multi-view on its TV app (not browser). Fubo costs more; if you only need it for the tournament, subscribe in June and cancel in July.

**You want commentary in a language other than English or Spanish.**
This is the gap. US-based streaming bundles do not surface Bengali, Hindi, Arabic, Portuguese, or other non-Spanish commentary feeds. BBC iPlayer (English-with-UK-feel commentary) and country-of-origin broadcasters like Star Sports India are geo-blocked from the US. VPN workarounds exist but are out of scope here.

**You're trying to follow USA + your country of origin at the same time.**
A combination tends to work better than picking one service. Common pairing: OTA antenna for FOX matches + Peacock Premium for Telemundo's Spanish-language schedule + Fubo or YouTube TV for the simultaneous-match days. Total cost ~$20-100 depending on whether you take Fubo only for July.

## Important caveats

- **Pricing changes monthly.** All numbers above are approximate as of 2026-05-23. Verify on each provider's site before subscribing.
- **FOX One is new and the pricing has not been published.** This is the wildcard — if FOX One ends up cheap enough, it could undercut Sling for English-only fans.
- **Multi-view feature support varies by device.** YouTube TV multi-view is TV-app only. Fubo multi-view works on TV and web. Verify on your specific TV / Roku / Apple TV / Fire TV before subscribing.
- **The FIFA Official App is not a streaming app.** It is for ticketing, schedule, and team news. Currently rated 1.8 stars on iOS (168 reviews) and 3.2 stars on Google Play (1,515 reviews). Do not rely on it for live match streams.
